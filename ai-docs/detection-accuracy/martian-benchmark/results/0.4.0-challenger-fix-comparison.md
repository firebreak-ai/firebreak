# Challenger Fix — 20-PR Comparison

**Date**: 2026-04-20
**Branch**: `feat/0.4.0-candidate-single-detector`
**Tool name**: `firebreak-v0.4.0-broad-challenger-fix`
**Scope**: same 20-PR balanced subset used for broad-behavioral run
**Judge**: 3x Sonnet 4.6 consensus (22/26/27 matches — wider spread than broad)

The Challenger instruction was updated to verify behavioral changes even when intent is ambiguous (note the ambiguity in evidence, don't reject), and to tighten the rejection criteria to "explicitly documented intent (specs, ACs, code comments) — inferences about what the author 'might have meant' are not grounds to reject." This run measures the incremental impact on top of the broadened behavioral type definition.

---

## Apples-to-apples (same 20 PRs)

| Config | TP | FP | FN | P | R | **F1** |
|--------|----|----|----|---|---|--------|
| Original baseline (corrected extract) | 24 | 54 | 31 | 30.8% | 43.6% | **36.1%** |
| + broadened behavioral | 27 | 54 | 28 | 33.3% | 49.1% | **39.7%** |
| + broadened + Challenger fix | **26** | **48** | 29 | **35.1%** | 47.3% | **40.3%** |

**Cumulative delta (Original → Fix):** F1 **+4.2pp** (36.1 → 40.3), Precision **+4.3pp**, Recall **+3.7pp**. Both axes improved.

**Fix-only delta vs broad:** F1 +0.6pp, Precision +1.8pp, Recall -1.8pp, **FP -6**. Precision-favorable.

## Notable per-repo patterns

**Discourse recovered** from broad's -13pp regression to +9.9pp over broad. The Challenger fix is especially beneficial where the broad definition over-eagerly produced findings the Challenger then had to triage. With the fix, the Challenger is more precise about what counts as "documented intent."

**Sentry lost broad's big gain.** Sentry went from 48.0% (broad, the best-performing repo under broad) down to 34.8% under fix. Possible explanation: broad was verifying sentry findings aggressively because the Challenger treated ambiguous intent as grounds to keep findings; the fix's tighter "your inference... is not grounds to reject" wording may have pushed the Challenger back toward rejecting findings it considered unclearly documented. The fix is designed to keep more findings, not fewer — something went the other way on sentry.

**Grafana partial recovery** (broad -6.6pp → fix -1.8pp vs original). Still below original.

## Inter-judge agreement

| Run | Matches across 3 judges |
|-----|-------------------------|
| Broad | 28, 28, 30 (spread: 2) |
| Fix | 22, 26, 27 (spread: 5) |

The fix introduced more judge variance. Likely cause: the "verify with intent ambiguity noted" guidance creates borderline cases where judges have to decide whether an ambiguously-documented finding counts as a match. When the Detector/Challenger produces findings like "this is a behavioral change but intent unclear," the judge has to assess if that matches a golden that makes a definite claim. Different judges decide differently.

Despite the spread, the majority-vote aggregate (26 TP) lands within the individual run range and close to broad's 27.

## What the fix did NOT solve (and what remains)

The fix addressed **intent-uncertainty rejection** but missed a related failure mode: **consequence-uncertainty rejection**. Example: PR22345's S-05 (findMany removal with potentially-broken callers) was rejected for "diff-only evidence gap — cannot confirm caller existence." This is NOT the fix's target pattern (intent is clear; callers may exist). PR22345 returned to 0 verified findings in the fix run (was 4 in broad).

This suggests the Challenger has a second conservative behavior that warrants a separate fix: when mechanism is verifiable but the full consequence chain requires extra-diff context, the Challenger should surface the finding with an evidence-gap caveat, not reject it.

## Caveats

- 20-PR sample; per-repo numbers are noisy with N=4.
- Two sources of variance stacking: orchestrator non-determinism + Challenger judgment borderline cases. Some per-PR deltas between broad and fix are likely noise rather than signal.
- The corrected baseline itself was derived from the same 50-PR review corpus that the broad-behavioral run was compared against. The reviews in the baseline weren't re-generated with the fix, only the broad-behavioral and fix subsets were fresh runs.

## Recommendation

Ship the broadened behavioral + Challenger fix together as the 0.4.0 candidate. The cumulative +4.2pp F1 gain is meaningful and applies across 4 of 5 repos. The sentry regression from broad → fix is concerning but sentry still nets +5.4pp vs original.

**Follow-up work** before v0.4.1 or further iteration:
1. Investigate Challenger consequence-uncertainty rejection (PR22345 pattern) — related but distinct from the fixed intent-uncertainty pattern.
2. Run a 50-PR benchmark under the full fix stack to tighten the headline and understand the sentry variance.
3. Review the sentry regression from broad → fix case-by-case to understand whether the Challenger tightening is correctly rejecting findings that shouldn't have been verified, or incorrectly tightening.
