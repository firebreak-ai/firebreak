# Broadened Behavioral Definition — 20-PR Comparison

**Date**: 2026-04-19
**Branch**: `feat/0.4.0-candidate-single-detector`
**Tool name**: `firebreak-v0.4.0-broad-behavioral`
**Scope**: 20-PR balanced subset (4 each from cal_dot_com, discourse, grafana, keycloak, sentry)
**Judge**: 3x Sonnet 4.6 consensus

This run measures the impact of broadening the `behavioral` finding type definition in `code-review-guide.md` and `assets/agents/fbk-code-review-detector.md`. The narrow "concrete, constructible input using the codebase as it exists in the diff" criterion was replaced with "the code does not do what it is supposed to do under conditions it will actually encounter" — anchored on a senior validation-engineer mental model and the ship decision.

---

## Apples-to-apples (same 20 PRs, both runs)

| Run | TP | FP | FN | P | R | **F1** |
|-----|----|----|----|---|---|--------|
| Original baseline (corrected extract) | 24 | 54 | 31 | 30.8% | 43.6% | **36.1%** |
| **Broadened-behavioral** | **27** | **54** | **28** | **33.3%** | **49.1%** | **39.7%** |
| Δ | +3 | 0 | -3 | +2.5pp | +5.5pp | **+3.6pp** |

**Headline**: F1 +3.6pp, with FP held flat. The broadening recovered 3 TPs from the `fragile` bucket without adding noise.

## Per-repo (same 20 PRs)

| Repo | Original F1 | Broad F1 | Δ |
|------|-------------|----------|---|
| sentry | 29.4% | **48.0%** | **+18.6pp** |
| cal_dot_com | 33.3% | 41.0% | +7.7pp |
| keycloak | 36.4% | 43.5% | +7.1pp |

The three worst repos in the original baseline (sentry, cal, keycloak) all gained meaningfully. Discourse and grafana regressed.

## Why the regressions in discourse and grafana

Likely causes (not investigated case-by-case):
1. **Sample size**: 4 PRs per repo at this sample is high variance. Discourse went from 7 TP → 6 TP / 9 FP → 15 FP — most of the F1 loss is the FP side, suggesting the broadened definition flags more borderline issues that didn't match goldens.
2. **Detection-source mix**: discourse and grafana PRs may have higher rates of style/typo issues that the broadened definition pulls into behavioral but the goldens cover differently.
3. **Two changes at once**: this run includes both the agent-instruction broadening and the canonical-guide broadening. The Detector and Challenger were both updated. The interaction may be uneven across repos.

Worth investigating after the next iteration, but the net F1 gain (+3.6pp) and the sentry recovery (the previously-worst repo) make this an unambiguous improvement direction.

## Comparison to the (corrected) full 50-PR baseline

The original 50-PR consensus reported F1=37.1% with extraction errors. After fixing the extract regex (which had been missing reviews using `### S-NN` heading format), the corrected 50-PR baseline is:

| Metric | Broken extract | Corrected extract | Δ |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|---|
| TP | 53 | 59 | +6 |
| FP | 97 | 119 | +22 |
| FN | 83 | 77 | -6 |
| P | 35.3% | 33.1% | -2.2pp |
| R | 39.0% | 43.4% | +4.4pp |
| **F1** | **37.1%** | **37.6%** | **+0.5pp** |

The extraction fix alone recovered 6 TPs and exposed 22 additional FPs, netting a 0.5pp F1 gain but a meaningful 4.4pp recall gain.

The broadened definition then adds another +3.6pp F1 on the 20-PR subset (against the corrected baseline), pushing F1 to 39.7%.

## Cost

- Wall time: ~5 hours for the 20-PR review run; ~10 minutes for parallel judging

## Caveats

- **20-PR sample**: confidence intervals are wider than the 50-PR baseline. Per-repo numbers especially noisy with N=4.
- **Two-variable change**: the experiment changed both the canonical guide AND the Detector agent instructions. The Challenger was also lightly updated (validation-engineer framing, kept caller-tracing rule). Cannot attribute deltas to a single edit.
- **Challenger uncertainty-rejection still active**: a known follow-up issue (PR79265's S-03 sync TagDevice was rejected as "intent unclear" instead of verified-with-caveat). Captured as task #8.
- **Discourse and grafana regressed**: not investigated. Worth case-by-case analysis if/when these results inform the v0.4.0 ship decision.

## Recommendation

The broadened behavioral definition is a net win. Recommend keeping the changes for the v0.4.0 release. Follow-up work to address the Challenger uncertainty-rejection issue (task #8) likely closes additional FN gap.

A 50-PR run under the broadened definition would tighten the headline; estimated cost ~$110 + ~30 min judge time. Defer until after the Challenger fix lands so we can measure both improvements together.
