# v0.4.0 Single-Detector Benchmark — 50 PRs

**Date**: 2026-04-19
**Branch**: `feat/0.4.0-candidate-single-detector`
**Tool name**: `firebreak-v0.4.0-single-detector`
**Scope**: All 50 PRs (10 each from cal_dot_com, discourse, grafana, keycloak, sentry)
**Config**: behavioral-only preset, single Detector + Challenger
**Judge**: Consensus (3x Sonnet 4.6, majority vote — judges agreed within 51-54 of 136 matches)
**Sandbox isolation**: claude invocations ran from `/tmp/firebreak-benchmark-sandbox` to prevent in-repo `ai-docs/` mis-routing

---

## Headline Results

| Version | PRs | TP | FP | FN | P | R | **F1** | Findings/PR |
|---------|-----|----|----|----|---|---|--------|-------------|
| v0.3.5 baseline (consensus) | 50 | 93 | 360 | 43 | 20.5% | 68.4% | **31.6%** | 9.0 |
| v0.4.0 decomposed (consensus) | 50 | 76 | 191 | 61 | 28.5% | 55.5% | **37.6%** | 5.3 |
| **v0.4.0 single-detector (this run)** | **50** | **53** | **97** | **83** | **35.3%** | **39.0%** | **37.1%** | **3.0** |
| v0.4.0 single-detector (23-PR sample) | 23 | 29 | 64 | 35 | 31.2% | 45.3% | 36.9% | 4.04 |

**Headline**: F1=37.1% essentially matches v0.4.0-decomposed (37.6%) at full scale, with a single Detector instead of the multi-detector decomposition. Precision climbs to 35.3%; recall drops to 39.0%. Output volume cut by 1/3 again (3.0 vs 5.3 findings/PR).

## Sample-Size Effects (23-PR vs 50-PR)

The 23-PR sample (cal_dot_com 7 + 4 each elsewhere) was slightly recall-favorable:
- Recall: 45.3% → 39.0% (-6.3pp at full scale)
- Precision: 31.2% → 35.3% (+4.1pp)
- F1: 36.9% → 37.1% (+0.2pp, essentially flat)

The fill-in PRs (especially the additional 6 sentry PRs) had goldens that the tool struggled to match. Sentry's 19.7% F1 is the largest drag on overall recall.

## Key Observations

### 1. Single-detector is competitive with decomposed

F1 differs by 0.5pp at full 50-PR scale. The decomposition's complexity premium is not paying off — a single Detector reaches the same accuracy with simpler orchestration. Decomposition's case for keeping it would need to come from a different metric (latency, debuggability, agent context budget) — none of which we measured here.

### 2. Sentry is the precision/recall floor

Sentry F1=19.7% drags the aggregate. 6 TP out of 31 goldens (19.4% recall). Looking at sentry PRs, many goldens are security/infra concerns (OAuth state predictability, auth bypass, integration metadata access) that the behavioral-only preset systematically routes through the `fragile` filter. The classification-definition issue identified during this run (see CHANGELOG entry on broadened behavioral) likely accounts for several of these misses.

### 3. Recall keeps dropping; precision keeps rising

Across releases: recall 68.4% → 55.5% → 39.0%. Precision: 20.5% → 28.5% → 35.3%. Each release tightens the filter further. At 39% recall, more than half of human-flagged issues are missed. The next lever needs to recover recall without sacrificing the precision gains — exactly what the broadened-behavioral definition is designed to do.

### 4. Ten zero-finding reviews

10 PRs (out of 50) produced no candidates: PR10967, PR22345 (cal.com), PR9, PR10 (discourse), PR107534 (grafana), PR33832 (keycloak), PR38446 (keycloak), PR77754, PR80168, PR80329 — wait, PR80329 had 4 candidates. Re-counting from the judge runs, the actual zero-candidate set is 10 PRs covering ~26 unmatched goldens. These PRs alone cap recall at ~80% (110/136) even before considering candidate-matching quality.

### 5. Inter-judge agreement is high

The 3 Sonnet judges produced 54, 53, and 51 matches respectively — 51-54 range across 136 goldens. Majority vote yields 53 TP. Agreement-rate suggests the 37.1% F1 estimate is stable; further judging passes would not materially change the headline.

## Caveats

- **Sample size**: full 50 PRs, no caveat — directly comparable to baseline
- **Judging methodology**: 3x Sonnet 4.6 majority vote vs baseline's 3x Opus. Sonnet judges may be marginally less generous in match acceptance, suppressing TP slightly. The relative comparison across versions remains valid because all use consensus protocols.
- **Behavioral type definition is being broadened in `code-review-guide.md`** (see CHANGELOG). A follow-up 20-PR sample under the new definition is expected to recover 5-8 TPs from the `fragile` bucket. Estimated post-update F1 lift: ~3-5pp.

## Conclusion

The single-detector candidate is **as accurate as the decomposed architecture** at full scale (F1 37.1% vs 37.6%) with substantially simpler orchestration. The architectural simplification is the right ship.

The recall floor is now the binding constraint. The just-landed broadened-behavioral definition targets one of the largest contributors to recall loss (genuine bugs misclassified as fragile and filtered out at the preset boundary). Whether that lifts F1 above 40% will be answered by the upcoming 20-PR rerun.
