## What the audit passes did NOT unlock

Five PRs are flat or regressed in TP:
- **keycloak PR36880** (logic-inv, findByType wrong-scope): 1 → 0 TPs, detector produced 0 candidates at all. True Detector miss.
- **grafana PR79265** (compile + concurrency): lost a TP. Three goldens uncaught.
- **sentry PR93824** (test-integrity): lost a TP. The test-integrity audit pass did not land.
- **sentry PR95633** (x-fn API): lost a TP. The cross-function API audit pass did not land.
- **cal.com PR22345** (logic-inv): flat at 1/5/1.

The test-integrity and cross-function-API audits did not produce measurable lift on this subset. Worth investigating whether the audit prompts actually fire in the Detector trace for these PRs, or whether the sightings generated are being rejected by the Challenger.

## Challenger rejection signal (new observation)

Three PRs in this run produced 0 verified findings (`####F-NN`) in the final review but had non-zero Detector sightings (`###S-NN`):

| PR | Sightings | Verified Findings | Judge TPs |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| cal.com PR8330 | 6 | 0 | **2** |
| sentry-gre PR5 | 7 | 0 | **1** |
| keycloak PR36880 | 0 | 0 | 0 |

The Detector found both goldens on PR8330 (including the dayjs `===` logic-inversion target) and one on sentry-gre PR5 — but the Challenger rejected every sighting. In production use, those TPs would be invisible to the end user. The judge saw the raw sightings via `extract_candidates.py`, so the TPs show up in the benchmark numbers anyway, but this is a concerning Challenger calibration signal worth a separate investigation.

## Comparison to retrospective's upper-bound estimate

The retrospective projected that combining the five low-to-medium buckets could lift recall from 47.8% → 58-62% on the full 50-PR set, a +10-14pp swing. On this 21-PR subset the audit passes delivered **+15.9pp recall** (37.7% → 53.6%). Subset comparison isn't directly proportional to the 50-PR projection (the subset over-samples the FN goldens by construction), but the direction matches and the magnitude exceeds the low end of the estimate.

## What this doesn't answer

- **Full 50-PR impact**: the other 29 PRs weren't re-run. If the audit passes produce FPs on PRs whose goldens they weren't targeting, aggregate precision could degrade more than this subset shows.
- **Challenger regression**: the 3 PRs where Sightings were completely rejected may be isolated to this run's detector style or may indicate Challenger over-rejection. Needs reproduction or a spot-check.
- **Per-audit-pass ablation**: the four audit passes were shipped together. This run cannot tell us which one is doing which work. The retrospective recommended one-variable-at-a-time evaluation; that's a multi-cycle budget call.

## Recommended next steps

1. **Re-run the remaining 29 PRs** (or the balanced-20 set) to verify precision doesn't collapse outside this subset.
2. **Spot-check Challenger rejections** on PR8330 and sentry-gre PR5 to see whether the Challenger is making individual bad calls or whether something about the new Detector output shape is triggering the rejection pathway.
3. **Investigate the four "flat" PRs** (PR36880, PR79265, PR93824, PR95633) where the targeted audit produced no lift — check the Detector trace for whether the relevant audit sub-prompt actually ran.
